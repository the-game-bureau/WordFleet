Rules of Engagement

Overview

Word Fleet combines the strategic depth of naval combat with the intellectual challenge of word puzzles. Two admirals command fleets of letter-ships, using vocabulary as their primary weapon system.

Fleet Composition

Each fleet consists of 5 word-ships of varying lengths:

  • Destroyer: 3-letter word
  • Submarine: 4-letter word
  • Cruiser: 5-letter word
  • Battleship: 6-letter word
  • Aircraft Carrier: 7-letter word

Setup Phase

Players secretly position their word-ships on their personal grid. Ships can be placed horizontally or vertically, but not diagonally. Ships cannot overlap or touch each other.

Combat Phase

Players take turns calling out words to attack enemy positions. If the word contains letters that match positions on the opponent's ships, those positions are marked as hits. A ship is sunk when all its letters have been hit.

Victory Conditions

The first admiral to sink all enemy word-ships claims victory and naval supremacy!

Strategic Notes

Choose your word-ships carefully - common letters make you vulnerable, but obscure words might be harder for you to remember under pressure. Balance offensive vocabulary with defensive positioning.

Optional Rules

  • Dictionary Challenge: Players may challenge words and consult a dictionary
  • Theme Fleets: All ships must follow a theme (animals, foods, etc.)
  • Radar Sweep: Once per game, reveal if a 3x3 area contains any ships
