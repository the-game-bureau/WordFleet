Word Fleet • Rules of Engagement

Objective

Your mission is to destroy your opponent's fleet. You and your opposing captain will secretly deploy word-ships across the high seas, then take turns launching attacks using letters and coordinates to locate and sink the enemy's fleet.

The Arrangement

Print battle trackers from thegamebureau.com/wrdflt. Both captains have 5 random 3-digit launch codes printed on their battle tracker.

  1. One captain boldly declares: "My highest launch code is 804!" (using any number from their sheet or making one up)
  2. The other captain can respond:
    • "You go first." (concede)
    • "You are a liar, Captain!" (challenge)
    • "I have a higher launch code of 900!" (raise the bid)
  3. Continue bidding until someone concedes or challenges
  4. Challenge Resolution: If you're caught lying, your opponent starts with two consecutive turns. If you told the truth, you start with two consecutive turns.
Defend

Setup your word-ships with tactical brilliance, because the bluffing is over - you must report your opponent's hits and misses honestly.

Deploy Your Fleet

Position your 5 word-ships by writing them in the grid, one letter per cell. Only write words left to right or top to bottom. Word-ships cannot overlap but may touch. Guard this section from enemy eyes during play.

  • KETCH: 5-letter word
  • SHIP: 4-letter word
  • SUB: 3-letter word
  • ARK: 3-letter word
  • PT: 2-letter word
Letter Count Manifest (Bottom Right)

This alphabet reference shows the count of each letter in your fleet and helps you quickly respond to enemy letter attacks. The manifest is pre-filled based on your chosen words, with vowels having shaded backgrounds for easy identification.

Attack

Now the real battle begins! Use intelligence, deduction, and tactical strikes to locate and destroy your opponent's hidden word-ships.

Fire!

Each turn has two phases: First, you may attempt to sink a word-ship by declaring its exact word and starting position (e.g., "B3: ARMOR"). If successful, the word-ship sinks immediately and you continue to Phase 2. If you fail, you lose your turn. In Phase 2, choose either a Coordinate Attack by calling a grid position (e.g., "D5") to get "Hit" or "Miss" and letter, or a Letter Attack by calling one letter (e.g., "R") to get the total count across all word-ships (e.g., "3 Rs"). Track your attacks on the Attack Grid (top left) - mark coordinate hits with large circles, letter hits with the letter, and misses with center dots. Fill in the bubble numbers as word-ships are sunk. Remember: each letter can only be called once per game.

Letter Count Recon (Top Right)

This alphabet grid tracks your letter attacks. Circle letters that score hits, cross out misses, and write hit counts in each tile for reference. Vowels have shaded backgrounds for quick identification.

Victory
The first captain to sink all of their opponent's word-ships wins!
Debrief
Strategic Notes
  • Choose your words carefully - common letters make you vulnerable to letter attacks, but obscure words might be harder to remember under pressure
  • Balance your vocabulary knowledge with tactical positioning on the grid
  • Use revealed information strategically - letter counts and coordinate hits can help deduce word patterns and orientations
  • Consider letter frequency when planning your attacks - vowels and common consonants appear more often
Game Variations

Dictionary Challenge: Challenge questionable words and consult an agreed-upon dictionary. Replace invalid words during fleet deployment.

Theme Fleets: All word-ships must follow a specific theme (animals, foods, naval terms, etc.).

Radar Sweep: Once per game, choose a 3×3 area and learn if it contains any word-ship parts (but not which letters or words).

Silent Running: Advanced captains may choose not to reveal specific hit locations when announcing letter attacks, providing only the total count.

Prepare for battle, Captain. Every letter is a weapon. Every word is a victory.
