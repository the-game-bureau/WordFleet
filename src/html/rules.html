WORD FLEET - Rules of Engagement

Word Fleet combines the strategic depth of naval combat with the intellectual challenge of word puzzles. Two admirals command fleets of letter-ships, using vocabulary as their primary weapon system.

FLEET COMPOSITION

Your fleet consists of 5 word-ships of varying lengths:

  • Aircraft Carrier: 5-letter word
  • Battleship: 4-letter word
  • Cruiser: 3-letter word
  • Destroyer: 3-letter word
  • Submarine: 2-letter word

SETUP PHASE

Secretly position your word-ships on your defense grid. Place ships horizontally or vertically only - words must read left-to-right (horizontal) or top-to-bottom (vertical). Ships cannot overlap or touch each other, even at corners.

DETERMINING FIRST STRIKE

Before battle begins, admirals must determine who fires first using the Launch Code Protocol:

  1. Opening Bid: One admiral declares their highest launch code digit (0-9)
  2. Response Options: The other admiral may:
    • Concede: "You may fire first" (bidding ends, first admiral attacks)
    • Challenge: "Prove it!" (proceed to verification)
    • Raise: Declare a higher number (bidding continues)
  3. Continued Bidding: Admirals alternate raising or challenging until someone concedes or challenges

Challenge Resolution:

  • The challenged admiral must reveal their launch code section (while hiding all other information)
  • Liar Caught: If they lied, the honest admiral gets TWO consecutive turns to start
  • False Accusation: If they told the truth, they get TWO consecutive turns to start
  • Note: An admiral may concede at any time to avoid being caught in a lie

COMBAT PHASE

Turn Structure

Each turn consists of two phases:

1. Word Declaration Phase (Optional)

  • Attempt to sink an enemy ship by declaring its exact word and starting position
  • State coordinates and spell the word (e.g., B3: ARMOR)
  • The word extends either horizontally or vertically from the starting position
  • Only one word declaration allowed per turn
  • You may skip this phase

2. Attack Phase (Choose One)

  • Coordinate Attack: Call specific grid coordinates (e.g., D5) - receive "Hit" or "Miss" with no letter information
  • Letter Attack: Call one letter (e.g., R) - receive total count across all ships, no location information (e.g., "3 Rs")

Important: Each letter can only be called once per game, even if multiple instances exist in the enemy fleet.

Attack Resolution

Word Declarations:

  • Correct: Ship immediately sinks, continue with your attack phase
  • Incorrect: Your turn ends immediately (forfeit attack phase)
  • Invalid Position: If the declared word doesn't fit on the grid from stated coordinates, your turn ends immediately

Coordinate Attacks:

  • Hit: Opponent says "Hit!" - no additional information given
  • Miss: Opponent says "Miss!"

Letter Attacks:

  • Hit: Opponent announces total count only (e.g., "3 Rs" or "1 Q")
  • Miss: Opponent says "0" or "None"

Ship Status

Ships sink when:

  • All letters are hit through letter calling, OR
  • The complete word is correctly declared with proper starting coordinates

VICTORY CONDITIONS

The first admiral to sink all enemy word-ships claims victory and naval supremacy!

STRATEGIC NOTES

  • Choose word-ships carefully - common letters make you vulnerable, but obscure words might be harder to remember under pressure
  • Balance offensive vocabulary knowledge with defensive positioning
  • Use revealed letter positions to deduce word patterns and ship orientations
  • Consider letter frequency when planning attacks

PEN & PAPER MODE

Battle Tracker Layout

Your Battle Tracker divides into three essential sections:

ATTACK GRID (Top Section)

  • Track attacks on the enemy fleet using this 10×10 grid (A-J, 1-10)
  • Mark hits with letters, misses with dots
  • Ship indicators (5-4-3-3-2) remind you of enemy fleet composition
  • "Fired Letters" manifest tracks which letters you've already called

MIDDLE SECTION

  • Contains your fleet name, launch codes, and optionally your word-ships
  • Launch codes determine who attacks first
  • Word-ships section helps you remember your fleet during battle

DEFENSE GRID (Bottom Section)

  • Your secret fleet positioning - guard this from enemy eyes!
  • Place your 5 word-ships on this 10×10 grid during setup
  • "Letter Manifest" shows each letter count in your fleet for quick reference during letter attacks

Attack Tracking

Fired Letters Manifest:

  • Circle letters that score hits, cross out letters that miss
  • Write the hit count in the letter tile for reference
  • Remember: each letter can only be called once per game

Attack Grid:

  • Mark coordinate attacks: write the letter for hits, place a dot for misses
  • Use gathered intelligence to plan word declarations

OPTIONAL RULES

Dictionary Challenge: Challenge questionable words and consult an agreed-upon dictionary. Replace invalid words during setup.

Theme Fleets: All ships must follow a specific theme (animals, foods, naval terms, etc.).

Radar Sweep: Once per game, choose a 3×3 area and learn if it contains any ship parts (but not which letters or words).

Silent Running: Advanced players may choose not to reveal specific locations when announcing letter hits, providing only the total count.

Prepare for battle, Admiral. Every letter is a weapon. Every word is a victory.
